Overview

Transportation is one of the largest sources of global carbon emissions. Flights are responsible for approximately 12% of global transport emissions and 2% of global carbon emissions while road transport (cars, buses, trucks, etc.) are responsible for 74% of global transport emissions and comprise of 20% of global carbon emissions. By carpooling, taking public transport, biking/walking to work, and reducing unnecessary air travel can significantly reduce your contribution to global warming.


The type of food that you ingest also contributes toward your overall carbon footprint. Those who eat meat use over 500 gallons of water more per day than those who eat a plant-based diet. This ultimately contributes to the rise of carbon emission because of the necessary steps and procedures to deliver certain ingredients to your table.


While the electricity in your home can greatly affect your carbon footprint, your phone and internet usage also increase your contribution to greenhouse gas emissions. Streaming music, videos, sending emails and texts, or calling a friend quickly adds up. Energy is needed to power the internet servers that run 24/7. Reduce your internet and call time to reduce your carbon footprint.


The fashion industry contributes 10% of global carbon emissions every year. Creating textiles from raw materials consumes large amounts of water and energy. By buying second hand or sustainably sourced clothing, you can help reduce your closet's carbon footprint.


Research has shown that recycling various items such as plastics water bottles, cans, and clothing are able to effectively reduce one's carbon footprint. By using reuseable watterbottles and straws, the amount of carbon emissions can be greatly lessened.


Our carbon footprint not only includes our transportation, diet, energy usage, and wardrobe, it also includes our water footprint. Water needs to be pumped and treated to make it usable by humans. By using less water, less energy and chemicals will be used, helping reduce our carbon emissions.
